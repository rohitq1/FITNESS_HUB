Dumbell curl


Equipment required:dumbbells

1.Stand up straight with a dumbbell in each hand at arm's length.

2.Raise one dumbbell and twist your forearm until it is vertical and your palm faces the shoulder.

3.Lower to original position and repeat with opposite arm

4.Complete 3 sets of 8–12 repetitions.

Single arm row


Equipment required: dumbbells

1.Make sure to keep your elbow close to your body and your core engaged throughout the movement.

2.Start by standing in front of a flat bench with your feet shoulder-width apart and holding a dumbbell in one hand.

3.Place your other hand on the bench for support, and bend at the waist so that your chest is parallel to the ground.

4.From this starting position, row the dumbbell up towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blade together as you do so.

5.Complete 3 sets of 8–12 repetitions.

Chin ups


Equipment required:pull up bar

1.Grab the bar shoulder width apart with a supinated grip palms facing you

2.With your body hanging and arms fully extended, pull yourself up until your chin is past the bar.

3.Slowly return to starting position. Repeat.

4.Complete 3 sets of 8–12 repetitions.

Dumbell hammer curls


Equipment required:dumbell

1.Hold the dumbbells with a neutral grip (thumbs facing the ceiling).

2.Slowly lift the dumbbell up to chest height

3.Aim to go as low as you can without losing the support of your core or the alignment of your spine and pelvis.

4.Return to starting position and repeat.

5.Repeat, completing 8–12 repetitions. Do 3 sets.

Barbell landmine row


Equipment required:barbell

1.Position a barbell in a landmine or corner of a room and stand facing it with your feet shoulder-width apart.

2.Grasp the end of the barbell with a neutral grip, keeping your arms straight and your shoulders relaxed.

3.Contract your chest muscles and bring the handles down and forward across your body at roughly belly button level. The hands can cross to add emphasis to the serratus anterior muscles.

4.Pull the barbell towards your chest, squeezing your shoulder blades together as you row the weight up.

5.Do 3 sets of 8–12 repetitions.
